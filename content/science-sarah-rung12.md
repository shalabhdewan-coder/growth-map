What the samples *do* contain (already used at rungs 6-8: Q8 solid-ID, Q9(i)-(iv) T/F sets) is a consistent HOTS reasoning style — multi-clue elimination, "which statement is actually true given constraints" — from the Achievers Section. That reasoning *style* (not a specific question) is what grounds this rung's new, harder entries: instead of "name the IV/DV" filler, the new `sc`/`method` items are flawed-experiment critiques and evidence-evaluation questions requiring the same multi-step elimination reasoning the SOF Achievers items model.

## What was added

- **No new vocab/facts** — hypothesis/IV/DV/CV terminology and its 4 facts are already correctly scoped; nothing new to source.
- **2 new `sc` entries** (was 1, the music/plant-height question): a flawed-experiment critique (two pot sizes AND two water amounts changed at once — student must identify why it's unfair and fix it), and a results-interpretation question (given a controlled hypothesis test with real numbers, decide whether the data supports or rejects the hypothesis and explain why). Both require holding multiple constraints in mind simultaneously, not single-fact recall.
- **2 new `method` entries** (was 1, the graph-description question): a full self-directed design task (state hypothesis + IV + DV + two controls, from scratch, for a new question), and an evidence-evaluation question (given a results table, identify what would need to have been controlled before trusting the data) — both push past "identify the variable in this given setup" into "construct/critique the whole experiment," which is the genuine escalation this rung needed.
