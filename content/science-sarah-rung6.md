# Sarah — Science — Rung 6 "States of matter & changes"

**Blended grade-equivalent target:** SG MOE Primary Science "Cycles in Matter and Water" (pp.21-22, upper block + P5/P6 foundation) + SOF-ISO Class 3 sample state-of-matter riddle.

## Source check

**`sources/science/moe-primary-science-syllabus-2008.pdf`** — Cycles in Matter and Water, verified outcomes:
- *"State that matter is anything that has mass and occupies space."*
- *"Differentiate between the three states of matter (solid, liquid, gas) in terms of shape and volume."*
- Melting/freezing at 0°C, boiling at 100°C: *"when ice is heated, it melts and changes to water at 0°C... when water is heated, it boils and changes to steam at 100°C."*
- *"Investigate the factors which affect the rate of evaporation... wind, temperature, exposed surface area."*

The rung's existing content (3 states, melting/evaporation/condensation vocab, sun-vs-shade drying) already tests the *temperature* evaporation factor. The other two MOE-named factors (wind, exposed surface area) were untested — that's the gap this update fills.

**`sources/science/sof-nso-iso-sample-class3.pdf`** — Q8, confirmed real: *"X has a definite volume and shape, but it cannot be compressed or poured. Identify X."* (Answer: wooden log, i.e. a solid). Reused near-verbatim as a state-of-matter riddle since it's directly on-topic and text-only (no image dependency).

## What was added

- **1 new vocab pair:** `matter` — MOE's own definition (anything with mass that occupies space), the umbrella term this whole rung sits under but wasn't previously defined.
- **1 new fact:** melting/freezing point of water (0°C) and boiling point (100°C), sourced directly to MOE's stated values.
- **2 new `sc` entries** (was 1): the SOF Class 3 Q8 solid-identification riddle (cited above, adapted to open-response), and an evaporation-factors question (windy+sunny puddle vs still+shady puddle) that names two of MOE's three evaporation-rate factors explicitly.
- **2 new `method` entries** (was 1, which already tested the *temperature* factor): a fan/no-fan fair test isolating the *wind* factor, and a wide-shallow-dish-vs-narrow-cup comparison isolating the *exposed surface area* factor. Together with the existing sun/shade item, all three MOE-named evaporation factors are now covered across the `method` bank.
