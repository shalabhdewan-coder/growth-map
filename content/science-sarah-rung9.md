# Sarah — Science — Rung 9 "The human body & systems"

**Blended grade-equivalent target:** SG MOE Primary Science "Human System" theme (p.24-25, Lower + Upper Block).

## Source check

**`sources/science/moe-primary-science-syllabus-2008.pdf`** (verified via `pdftotext -layout`) — Systems theme, Human System, confirmed outcomes:
- Lower block: *"Identify the organ systems in human (digestive, respiratory, circulatory, skeletal and muscular)."* / *"Identify the organs in the human digestive system and describe their functions."*
- Upper block: *"Identify the organs of the human respiratory and circulatory systems and describe their functions."* / *"Recognise the integration of the different systems (digestive, respiratory and circulatory) in carrying out life processes"* — skill: *"Compare the ways in which nutrients, water and oxygen are transported within plants and humans... humans: blood vessels that transport digested food and other substances."*

This is real, on-topic MOE content and directly names five organ systems. Current content only tested three of the five (digestive, respiratory, circulatory) and never tested MOE's explicit "integration" outcome — the gap this update fills. This is the highest rung where MOE's Human System theme applies; it is genuinely Upper Block (P5/P6) material, so age-appropriate at this ladder position, confirming the plan's expectation that primary MOE reaches this far but no further (no anatomy beyond organ-system level).

## What was added

- **2 new vocab pairs:** `skeletal system`, `muscular system` — MOE's own two remaining named organ systems, previously missing from the bank.
- **1 new fact:** naming all five MOE-listed organ systems together (digestive, respiratory, circulatory, skeletal, muscular).
- **2 new `sc` entries** (was 1): an integration-reasoning question (which system carries both digested nutrients and inhaled oxygen to every cell — circulatory, via blood vessels), sourced directly to MOE's "integration of systems" outcome; and a skeletal+muscular question (elbow bending) sourced to MOE's two lower-block-named systems that weren't yet covered.
- **2 new `method` entries** (was 1, a pulse fair test): a pulse-comparison fair test across two exercise intensities (isolating intensity as the one changed variable), and a breathing-rate fair test extending the existing pulse-only method to also cover the respiratory system, both following MOE's own "compare/investigate" skill pattern already used elsewhere in this ladder.
