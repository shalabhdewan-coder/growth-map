# Sarah — Maths — Rung 10 "Algebra & geometry"

**Blended grade-equivalent target:** Lower-secondary / Math Kangaroo Benjamin (entry tier) — per the plan's rung-map table, this is where formal single-variable equation solving (`mx+c=value`) and standalone geometry (area/perimeter/angle-sum) both become live for the first time. The task explicitly flagged `m_algebra` and `m_geometry` as "newly relevant at rung 10 — audit those specifically, they haven't been touched in this whole project," so this rung's audit goes deeper into those two generators than any prior rung's note.

**`sources/maths/open-middle/open-middle-index.md`** (already-indexed official Open Middle catalogue) — confirms an "Area & Perimeter (Grade 3-6)" collection explicitly mapped to "Sarah rung 10 geometry" in the index, including **"Rectangles: Maximizing Area"** (draw three rectangles with perimeter 20, maximize area) and a fixed-area/minimize-perimeter counterpart. **`sources/maths/amc8-compendium-1985-2026.pdf`** — 2013 AMC 8 P5 (Hammie/quadruplets mean-vs-median) was already sourced and wired into `m_words`' `algebra>=2` branch during the rung 12 task; confirmed here that this branch already correctly reaches rung 10 (any rung with `algebra>=2` gets it) — verified, no change needed, see audit below.

## Verdict on current knobs/generators — dead-flag / narrow-reach audit

`LEVELS.sarah.maths[9]` (before this task) `= {n:'Algebra & geometry', algebra:2, geom:1, s:3, bar:'pct', patt:'quad', chal:5}`.

**Severity-1 finding — a real dead-flag bug spanning rungs 10, 11 AND 12, not just this rung:** rungs 6 through 9 all carry `longmul:1` forward as continued advanced-fact-fluency practice (confirmed in every prior rung's note). **Rung 10's knob object had no `longmul` at all — and neither did rung 11's or rung 12's (already-built).** Traced this through `m_warm`: the `specials` array is built from `if(L.frac)…if(L.longmul)…if(L.mul2)…if(L.dec)…if(L.ratio)…if(L.pct)…` — rung 10 (pre-fix) sets **none** of these, so `specials` was an **empty array**, meaning `(pick===0&&specials.length)` was always false and the warm-up block could **only ever produce plain ×3-12 fact-fluency or division facts — identical in difficulty to rung 1** — for what should be the second-hardest rung on the entire ladder. This is the single most consequential finding of this session: the warm-up block (the very first thing on every sheet) was silently regressing to the easiest possible content for the top three rungs of the ladder, which plausibly explains any sense that "rung 11 feels barely different from rung 8" — the first block a user sees on both would have looked identical. **Fixed by restoring `longmul:1` to rungs 10, 11, and 12's knob objects** (documented per-rung; rung 12's re-verified clean after this ripple, see Verification).

Traced every reference to `L.algebra` and `L.geom` specifically for this rung's combination, per the task's explicit instruction to audit `m_algebra`/`m_geometry`:
- **`m_algebra`**: `algebra>=1` and `algebra>=2` branches both fire correctly for rung 10 (4 items total: two "unknown box" items plus two `mx±c=value` items). Well-calibrated, matches "formal single-variable equation solving" as this rung's headline new skill. **However**: rungs 10, 11, and 12 all set `algebra:2` identically (pre-fix) — meaning `m_algebra`'s output was **literally identical in difficulty across three consecutive rungs**, with zero escalation from rung 10 through rung 12 in this block. This directly contradicts the "smooth escalation" requirement. **Fixed**: added a new `algebra>=3` tier containing a genuine magic-square item (the NRICH "Partial Magic" analogue described above — see "Changes made"), and bumped rung 11 to `algebra:3`. Rung 10 stays at `algebra:2` (the correct entry-tier boundary — Math Kangaroo Benjamin entry, not upper), so rung 10 → 11 now shows a real, sourced escalation in this block, while rung 12 was also bumped to `algebra:3` (see rung 11's note) so it remains a strict superset rather than falling behind rung 11.
- **`m_geometry`**: was `function m_geometry()` — **took no arguments at all**, meaning it had zero knowledge of which rung called it and could not possibly differentiate rung 10's geometry from rung 11's or rung 12's. Its three hardcoded options (rectangle area/perimeter, triangle missing-angle, triangle area) were identical regardless of rung — confirmed by the placement-assessment's own honest gap noted above (composite-shape decomposition, Sarah's actual routine graded-work evidence, had no matching option at all). **Fixed**: changed the signature to `m_geometry(L)` (call site in `ws_maths_sarah` updated to `m_geometry(L)`), added a 4th option (composite L-shape area, available at every geometry rung including rung 10 — matches Sarah's routine graded evidence directly, not gated behind `comp`) and a 5th option (shaded/subtracted-region area, gated on `L.comp` so it only appears from rung 11 onward — a genuine escalation, modeled on the AMC8-style "area of the region NOT covered" framing already catalogued in rung 12's note and the Open Middle area/perimeter collection's optimization framing).
- **`m_words`**: `algebra>=2` branch (mean/median problem, added during the rung 12 task) already correctly reaches rung 10 — verified, no change needed here.
- **`m_bar`** (`bar:'pct'`) and **`m_mistake`** (`pctBank` doesn't apply here since rung 10 doesn't set `pct` — falls to the `hard` bank plus the new `algebraBank` from rung 9's fix): both reviewed, both correctly reached, no rung-10-specific change needed beyond the `longmul`/`algebra` fixes already described.
- **`m_pattern`** (`patt:'quad'`): found to be **completely non-randomized** — `const s=[2,6,12,20];return{seq:s,next:[30,42],rule:'n × (n+1) …'};` was a hardcoded constant with **zero use of `R()`**, meaning every single worksheet generated for rungs 10, 11, and 12 (all three set `patt:'quad'`) showed the **exact same four numbers every time**, forever. This is a distinct bug from the knob-level "dead flag" class but is the same root problem (a generator silently ignoring variation it should have) and directly undermines "does this feel like a real, fresh ladder" for the three rungs where a user would spend the most repeated practice time. **Fixed**: added a random offset `c=R(1,4)` so the rule becomes `position × (position + c)`, varying every generation while staying quadratic-difficulty-equivalent.

## Changes made in `growth-map.html`

1. **`LEVELS.sarah.maths[9]`**: added `longmul:1` (restores advanced warm-up fluency, fixing the severity-1 finding above).
2. **`m_geometry`**: signature changed from `()` to `(L)`; added composite-L-shape option (opt 3, always available whenever `geom` is set) and shaded-region option (opt 4, gated on `L.comp`, so rung 10 draws from 4 options and rung 11/12 draw from 5).
3. **`m_algebra`**: added `algebra>=3` tier (magic-square item) — does not affect rung 10 (`algebra:2`), only rungs 11-12 (bumped to `algebra:3`, see rung 11's note for the generator detail).
4. **`m_pattern`**: `quad` branch randomized (`position × (position + c)`, `c=R(1,4)`) instead of a hardcoded constant — affects rungs 10, 11, 12 equally (all three share `patt:'quad'`), a pure variety fix with no difficulty-tier implications.
5. **`ws_maths_sarah`**: call site updated from `m_geometry()` to `m_geometry(L)`.
6. **`mLevelTag`**: rung 10's on-screen label text updated from the vague "P6 + a taste of SASMO / geometry" to "Lower-secondary · algebra & geometry (Math Kangaroo Benjamin)" so it actually names the sourced calibration target.

No existing function signatures changed beyond the intentional, documented `m_geometry` extension (additive — old callers passing no argument still work, `L` simply defaults to `undefined` and the ternary falls back to the base 4-option pool). `ws_maths_sarah`'s own call signature is untouched.
