# Mirayah — Science — Rung 7 "Push & pull"

**Blended grade-equivalent target:** pre-formal-science (age 6) foundation for MOE's Lower Block "Interactions of Forces" outcome — genuinely close, not one band up in spirit even though it's still Primary 3-4 vocabulary.

## Source check

**`sources/science/moe-primary-science-syllabus-2008.pdf`** (p.27-28, "Interactions of Forces", Lower Block) — real learning outcomes: *"Recognise that a magnet can exert a push or a pull"* and *"Identify the characteristics of magnets — magnets can be made of iron or steel; magnets have two poles; unlike poles attract and like poles repel; magnets attract magnetic materials."* This is a strong, close match for a push/pull rung — magnets are literally taught as an example of push-and-pull force at Lower Block. The existing rolling-ball/friction item is *not* from this section (friction/gravity are Upper Block "Interaction of forces" content, p.42) but is simple enough general physical intuition to keep as-is, one age-band up from where MOE formally introduces it.

**`sources/science/sof-nso-iso-sample-class1.pdf`** — grepped for "push|pull|magnet|force": no citable text question on this theme in the Class 1 sample.

## Verdict

Genuinely well-sourced rung — magnets-as-push/pull is real Lower Block MOE content, simplified for age 6 (no poles/N-S vocabulary, just "does it pull or push").
