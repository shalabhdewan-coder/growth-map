### Rectangle Tangle — full text
"The large rectangle above is divided into a series of smaller quadrilaterals and triangles. Each of the shapes is a fractional part of the large rectangle." Task: identify what fractional portion each of ten numbered shapes represents within the whole, explain reasoning. Getting-started prompts: "What shapes have you found? How big is this shape? Which is the biggest/smallest shape? Tell me how you found this out?" Extension: "create their own problems for others to solve."

## Multiplication & Division reasoning — Age 7-11
Source: https://nrich.maths.org/ratio-and-proportion-age-7-11 (NRICH's own tag routing — despite the URL name this collection is multiplicative-reasoning/number-property focused) — maps to Sarah rungs 6-7 (Multiplication mastery, Ratio & proportion)

Standouts for algebra-adjacent reasoning (good for rung 9 pre-algebra bridge):
- **Shape Times Shape** (https://nrich.maths.org/problems/shape-times-shape) — "These eleven shapes each stand for a different number. Can you use the number sentences to work out what they are?" — simultaneous-equations-by-symbol, no formal algebra notation needed.
- **Zias and Zepts** (https://nrich.maths.org/problems/zias-and-zepts) — "The Zias have 3 legs and the Zepts have 7 legs. The great planetary explorer Nico counted 52 legs." — classic two-variable word problem, algebra-lite.
- **Satisfying Four Statements** (https://nrich.maths.org/problems/satisfying-four-statements) — "Can you find any two-digit numbers that satisfy all of these statements?" — constraint-logic, matches Sarah's rung-12 logic-deduction block style.
- **Abundant Numbers** (https://nrich.maths.org/problems/abundant-numbers) — number theory (factor sums).
- **Multiply Multiples 1/2/3, Digit Discovery, Four-Digit Targets, What's in the Box?** — missing-digit / inverse-operation puzzles, good rung 6-8 fit.

## Patterns and Sequences — Age 11-14
Source: https://nrich.maths.org/patterns-and-sequences-stage-3 — maps to Sarah's `patt` knob (rungs using patt:'seq'/'quad', i.e. rungs 7-9) and rung 10-11 (Algebra & geometry / Competition heuristics)

Standouts:
- **Summing Consecutive Numbers** (https://nrich.maths.org/problems/summing-consecutive-numbers) — "Can you say which numbers can be expressed as the sum of two or more consecutive integers?"
- **Picturing Triangular Numbers** / **Picturing Square Numbers** — visual proof-lite pattern reasoning, matches rung 12's proof block style.
- **Growing Surprises** (https://nrich.maths.org/problems/growing-surprises) — "Can you find the connections between linear and quadratic patterns?" — directly matches `patt:'quad'` rungs.
- **Charlie's Delightful Machine** (https://nrich.maths.org/problems/charlies-delightful-machine) — "Can you develop a strategy to work out the rules controlling each light?" — inverse-logic/deduction, rung 11-12 caliber.
- **Frogs, Tower of Hanoi, 1 Step 2 Step** — classic combinatorial/recursive puzzles, rung 11-12 (competition heuristics).
- **Coordinate Patterns, Squares in Rectangles, Triangle Numbers, Beach Huts, Seven Squares** — sequence/geometric-pattern problems, rungs 7-9.

## Divisibility / Number Theory
Source: https://nrich.maths.org/tags/divisibility — maps to rung 12's logic/CRT-remainder-style content.
